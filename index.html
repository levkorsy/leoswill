Spring: Cherry Blossom Old Fashioned

Spring is all about renewal and fresh beginnings. The Cherry Blossom Old Fashioned captures the light and floral notes of the season, perfect for enjoying the blossoming beauty around you.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey (recommended: Buffalo Trace Bourbon or Bulleit Rye)
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 bar spoon of cherry syrup
  • Orange twist
  • Metal whiskey stones
  • Cherry wood chips (using the provided wood chip tongs)

Instructions:

1. Preparation:

  • Muddle the sugar cube with the bitters and cherry syrup in a mixing glass until the sugar is dissolved.
  • Add the whiskey and stir to combine.

2. Smoking the Glass:

  • Smoker Setup:Place the smoker on an empty glass and attach the metal filter
  • Wood Chips:Use tongs to place a thin layer of cherry wood chips just enough to cover the bottom of the smoker chamber.
  • Torch:Hold the torch at a 90-degree angle and light the wood chips for 5-7 seconds until they start to char and produce smoke.
  • Smoke Time:Cover with the smoker lid for 1-2 minutes to infuse smoke flavor.
  • Handling Safety:Carefully remove the smoker, avoiding contact with the hot metal filter.

3. Final Assembly:

  • Quickly add the prepared whiskey mixture from the mixing glass into the smoked glass
  • Add metal whiskey stones and stir until well mixed.
  • Garnish with an orange twist.

Smoking the Whiskey (Alternative):

  • Pour the prepared whiskey mixture into a glass suitable for smoking.
  • Smoker Setup:Place the smoker on top of the glass and attach the metal filter.
  • Wood Chips:Use tongs to place a thin layer of cherry wood chips just enough to cover the bottom of the smoker chamber.
  • Torch:Hold the torch at a 90-degree angle and light the wood chips for 5-7 seconds until they start to char and produce smoke.
  • Smoke Time:Cover with the smoker lid for 1-2 minutes to infuse smoke flavor.
  • Handling Safety:Carefully remove the smoker, avoiding contact with the hot metal filter.
  • Add metal whiskey stones and stir until well mixed.
  • Garnish with an orange twist

Preferred Whiskey:

  • Buffalo Trace Bourbon:A well-rounded bourbon with rich caramel and vanilla notes, perfect for an Old Fashioned.
  • Bulleit Rye:Known for its spiciness and complexity, it adds a unique depth to the cocktail.
  • Your favorite bourbon or rye whiskey can also be used.

Detailed Instructions for Using a Cocktail Smoker

To ensure a seamless and enjoyable experience with your cocktail smoker, follow these detailed instructions:

Smoker Setup:

  1. Place the smoker on an empty glass and attach the metal filter securely.

Wood Chips:

  1. Use tongs to place a thin layer of wood chips (such as cherry, applewood, hickory, or oak) in the smoker chamber. Do not overfill; a small amount is sufficient to produce the desired smoke.

Torch:

  1. Hold the torch at a 90-degree angle. Ignite the wood chips by applying the flame directly to the chips for 5-7 seconds until they start to char and produce smoke.
    • Ensure to handle the torch safely and avoid direct contact with the wooden parts of the smoker to prevent damage.

Smoke Time:

  1. Cover the glass with the smoker lid and let the smoke infuse for 1-2 minutes. The longer you leave it covered, the more intense the smoky flavor will be.

Handling Safety:

  1. Carefully remove the smoker, avoiding contact with the hot metal filter to prevent burns.

Pour Whiskey:

  1. Immediately pour your prepared whiskey or cocktail into the smoked glass. This step ensures that the smoky flavor integrates well with the drink.

Metal Ice Cubes:

  1. Rinse and dry the metal cubes, place them in a velvet pouch, and chill in the refrigerator for 1-2 hours before use. Metal cubes are preferred over ice to avoid dilution of the cocktail.

Serving:

  1. Add the chilled metal cubes directly to the whiskey or cocktail in the smoked glass and enjoy your drink.

Additional Tips:

  • Experiment with Smoking Times: Start with a shorter infusion time and adjust based on your taste preference for smokiness.
  • Types of Wood Chips: Different woods impart different flavors. Cherry wood chips, for instance, are excellent for a smooth, sweet note, while oak chips provide a robust, rich flavor.
  • Cleaning: Ensure you clean the smoker after each use. Knock loose any wood chips or charring from the screen and wipe the wooden parts with a dry or damp cloth as needed. Use the provided brush to clean the metal filter. Avoid submerging the smoker in water.

These instructions should help you make the most of your cocktail smoker and create impressive, flavorful drinks. Enjoy your mixology adventure!

Detailed Instructions for Using Metal Whiskey Stones with Gel Inside

Setup and Usage:

1. Preparation:

  • Rinse the metal whiskey stones with water to remove any residue from storage.
  • Dry the stones thoroughly before chilling.

2. Chilling:

  • Place the clean and dry metal whiskey stones in the velvet pouch provided.
  • Put the pouch in the freezer for at least 2 hours before use. The gel inside the stones will freeze, ensuring they stay cold for a long time.

3. Using the Whiskey Stones:

  • Once chilled, add 2-3 stones to your glass of whiskey, cocktail, or any preferred beverage.
  • Let the stones sit in the drink for a few minutes to chill it without diluting the flavor.
  • Enjoy your perfectly chilled drink!

Advantages of Metal Whiskey Stones over Regular Ice Cubes:

1. Non-Diluting:

  • Metal Stones: Metal whiskey stones do not melt, which means they chill your drink without watering it down. This preserves the original flavor and strength of your beverage.
  • Ice Cubes: As ice cubes melt, they dilute the drink, altering its taste and reducing its potency.

2. Long-Lasting Cooling:

  • Metal Stones: The gel inside metal whiskey stones freezes and stays cold for an extended period, providing consistent cooling throughout your drinking experience.
  • Ice Cubes: Ice cubes can melt relatively quickly, especially in warm environments, reducing their effectiveness over time.

3. Material and Safety:

  • Metal Stones: Made from food-grade stainless steel, metal whiskey stones are safe, durable, and resistant to corrosion. They do not impart any off flavors or odors to the drink.
  • Ice Cubes: Ice cubes are made from water and can sometimes carry impurities or flavors from the freezer.

4. Reusable:

  • Metal Stones: Metal whiskey stones are reusable. Simply rinse them after use, dry, and place them back in the freezer for the next use.
  • Ice Cubes: Ice cubes are single-use and need to be replaced for each drink, requiring a constant supply of clean water and freezer space.

5. Aesthetic Appeal:

  • Metal Stones: The sleek, modern look of stainless steel stones adds a touch of elegance to any drink, making them a perfect addition for entertaining guests or for personal enjoyment.
  • Ice Cubes: Traditional ice cubes are practical but lack the visual appeal of metal whiskey stones.

6. Enhanced Cooling Efficiency:

  • Metal Stones: The gel core inside the stones enhances their cooling capacity, making them more efficient at chilling beverages compared to regular solid metal stones.
  • Ice Cubes: Ice cubes provide effective cooling but melt over time, which can lead to inconsistent temperature control.

Versatility: Where and How to Use Metal Whiskey Stones:

1. At Home:

  • Perfect for chilling whiskey, bourbon, scotch, and other spirits without dilution.
  • Use them in cocktails to maintain the integrity of mixed drinks.

2. At Parties:

  • Impress guests with elegant, non-diluting cooling stones in their drinks.
  • Use them in wine and champagne glasses to keep beverages cold without watering them down.

3. Outdoor Events:

  • Ideal for picnics and BBQs where ice might melt quickly.
  • Great for camping trips to keep your beverages cold without the hassle of ice.

4. In the Office:

  • Keep a set in the office freezer to enjoy a perfectly chilled drink after a long day.
  • Use them to cool non-alcoholic beverages like iced tea, soda, or juice.

5. Travel:

  • Easy to pack and carry for vacations or business trips.
  • Enjoy chilled drinks in your hotel room without relying on hotel ice.

Cleaning and Maintenance:

1. Post-Use Cleaning:

  • After use, rinse the stones under warm water to remove any beverage residue.
  • Dry them thoroughly with a soft cloth or allow them to air dry.

2. Storage:

  • Store the clean and dry stones in their velvet pouch. This keeps them free from dust and ready for the next use.

3. Avoid Submersion in Water:

  • While the stones are designed to be durable, avoid submerging them in water for prolonged periods, as this can affect the gel core.
By following these instructions and understanding the benefits, you can maximize your enjoyment of using metal whiskey stones with gel inside. Cheers to a perfectly chilled drink!
Tips for Proper Smoking with a Cocktail Smoker: Glass First vs. Whiskey First

Smoking the Glass First: The Subtle Touch

Imagine your first sip coming with a whisper of smoke, enhancing the aromas and creating a perfect prelude to the flavors of your cocktail. Here’s why you might want to start with the glass:

Why Smoke the Glass?

  • Balanced Flavor: Smoking the glass gives your drink a gentle smoky flavor. The smoke lingers in the glass and mixes slowly with your cocktail, making sure it doesn’t overpower the drink.
  • Aromas Galore: When you smoke the glass, the aroma hits you even before you take a sip. This creates an immersive sensory experience, engaging your sense of smell along with taste.
  • Visual Impact: Watching the smoke swirl around the glass as you pour your drink adds a dramatic, visual appeal that impresses guests and elevates your cocktail presentation.

How to Smoke the Glass:

  • Set up the smoker on an empty glass.
  • Use tongs to place a small amount of wood chips in the smoker chamber.
  • Ignite the chips with a torch for 5-7 seconds until they start to smoke.
  • Cover the glass with the smoker lid for 1-2 minutes.
  • Carefully remove the smoker, avoiding the hot metal parts.
  • Pour your prepared cocktail into the smoked glass.

Smoking the Whiskey First: The Bold Move

For those who love a rich, intense flavor, smoking the whiskey itself is the way to go. This method infuses the drink with a deep, smoky taste that transforms every sip.

Why Smoke the Whiskey?

  • Intense Flavor: Smoking the whiskey directly results in a stronger, more pronounced smoky flavor. It’s perfect for those who love a bold taste that stands out.
  • Deeper Infusion: The smoke penetrates the whiskey thoroughly, creating layers of complexity and richness in every sip.
  • Even Flavor: Smoking the whiskey ensures the smoky flavor is evenly distributed, so each sip is consistent and delicious.

How to Smoke the Whiskey:

  • Pour your whiskey or cocktail into a suitable glass.
  • Place the smoker on top of the glass.
  • Add a small amount of wood chips to the smoker chamber.
  • Ignite the chips with a torch for 5-7 seconds until they start to smoke.
  • Cover the glass with the smoker lid for 1-2 minutes.
  • Carefully remove the smoker, avoiding the hot metal parts.
  • Enjoy your richly smoked drink.

Whether you prefer the subtle, aromatic allure of smoking the glass or the bold, intense flavor of smoking the whiskey, each method offers a unique way to enjoy your cocktails.

  • Smoke the Glass First for a mild, aromatic experience that enhances the sensory appeal and adds a touch of elegance to your drink.
  • Smoke the Whiskey First for a bold, rich flavor that transforms your cocktail into a smoky masterpiece.

Experiment with both methods to discover your favorite. Cheers to creating perfect smoky cocktails that will impress your friends and delight your taste buds!
